Production Engineering Research Project on a Sheet Forming Process - Manipal University (2016)

At the end of my engineering degree, I carried out a major research project on a process modern and complex process called Incremental Sheet Forming. It is widely used to accurately form sheet metals in aircrafts, vehicles and on any machinery that requires high level of build accuracy.

  • The project aimed to model equations that predict friction for a sheet metal process.
  • Implementing original prototyping and analysis techniques, conducting lab experiments and leveraged observed data.
  • With the help of processing of data on MATLAB, I derived a set of equations that calculated the forces with a worst deviation being just 27%.
  • In most cases, the deviations were stable and less than 5% from experimental values.

This achievement was unprecedented as the deviations using older methods were upto 65% inaccurate in certain cases.
